GenAI Methods for Tumor Evolution and Early Detection

Pioneering the application of Large Language Models and generative AI to understand cancer progression patterns and enable early detection through electronic health records, revolutionizing both basic cancer biology research and clinical decision-making.

Research Overview

Our lab is at the forefront of developing and applying Generative AI and Large Language Models (LLMs) to tackle two critical challenges in cancer research: understanding tumor evolution at the single-cell level and enabling early detection of pancreatic cancer through electronic health record analysis. By treating genomic data as a language and leveraging transformer architectures, we are creating powerful tools that can both predict cancer progression patterns and identify high-risk patients years before clinical symptoms appear.

Dual Impact: Basic Science and Clinical Translation

This integrated research program represents a paradigm shift in cancer genomics, combining foundational advances in understanding tumor evolution with practical clinical applications for early detection. Our LLM-based approaches enable zero-shot prediction of cancer progression, mechanistic interpretability of tumor biology, and the potential to dramatically improve patient outcomes through early intervention.

Part I: LLMs for Tumor Evolution

evoCancerGPT - Zero-shot Cancer Evolution

evoCancerGPT is our flagship large language model specifically designed to understand and generate cancer evolutionary trajectories. Using a decoder-only transformer architecture, the model can predict the next steps in cancer progression given initial cellular states, treating cancer evolution as a narrative that can be both understood and predicted.

evoCancerGPT Architecture Diagram

Decoder-only transformer with MLP layers for sparse expression matrices

Figure: Overview of evoCancerGPT architecture showing data preprocessing pipeline, sentence construction from 4096 tokens representing cells in pseudotime order, and transformer architecture with extra MLP layers to handle sparse scRNA-seq data and non-linear relationships.
Model Architecture
  • Decoder-only transformer with 4096 token context length
  • Evolutionary sentences constructed in increasing pseudotime order
  • Extra MLP layers before and after transformer blocks
  • Specialized handling of sparse expression matrices
  • Zero-shot generation capabilities for unseen cancer types

Multi-modal Foundation Models for Genomics

We are developing the next generation of foundation models that can simultaneously process and integrate multiple types of genomic data - transcriptomics, proteomics, and epigenomics - within a unified transformer architecture. This multi-modal approach enables comprehensive understanding of tumor biology.
Technical Approach
  • Multi-modal transformer architecture with cross-attention mechanisms
  • Custom tokenization strategies for different omics data types
  • Self-supervised pre-training on large-scale cancer genomics datasets
  • Fine-tuning for downstream tasks including drug response prediction
  • Handling missing modalities common in clinical settings

Foundation Models for Clinical Trial Data

We evaluate and apply state-of-the-art foundation models like scGPT to real-world single nucleus RNA sequencing data from ongoing clinical trials at the Hale Center for Pancreatic Cancer. This work bridges the gap between research models and clinical applications.
Clinical Trial Integration
  • Analysis of Paricalcitol clinical trial data (Vitamin D receptor agonist)
  • Pre-treatment and post-treatment single nucleus RNA sequencing
  • Foundation model fine-tuning on clinical genomics data
  • Biomarker discovery for treatment response prediction
  • Integration with clinical outcome data

Reinforcement Learning Enhanced LLMs

We implement reinforcement learning from human feedback (RLHF) techniques to improve LLM performance on single cell genomics tasks, particularly cell type annotation. This approach, inspired by our collaboration with DeepSeek-R1, enhances model reasoning capabilities.
Reinforcement Learning Framework
  • Human feedback integration for cell type annotation quality
  • Reward model training based on expert biological knowledge
  • Policy optimization for improved reasoning chains
  • Multi-step reasoning for complex cell state identification
  • Continuous learning from expert corrections

Part II: LLMs for Early Cancer Detection

The Pancreatic Cancer Early Detection Challenge

Pancreatic cancer has a 5-year survival rate of only 12%, primarily because 80% of patients are diagnosed at advanced stages when surgical resection is no longer possible. Early detection could dramatically improve outcomes, as patients diagnosed at early stages have survival rates approaching 70%. Our LLM-based approach to analyzing electronic health records could identify high-risk patients 1-3 years before clinical diagnosis.

Mass Gen-Brigham-Dana Farber Electronic Health Records

We have access to electronic health records from 1 million patients in the Mass Gen-Brigham-Dana Farber medical system, representing one of the largest and most comprehensive healthcare datasets available for pancreatic cancer research. This dataset includes decades of patient interactions across multiple healthcare settings.

Adapting Foundation Models for EHR Analysis

Led by Dr. Jifan Gao (joining June 2025), we are developing novel Large Language Model architectures specifically designed for electronic health record analysis. This work builds on recent breakthroughs in medical AI while addressing the unique challenges of pancreatic cancer early detection.
Technical Innovation
  • Adaptation of transformer architectures for temporal medical data
  • Multi-modal integration of text, numerical, and imaging data
  • Attention mechanisms for long-term temporal relationships
  • Handling missing and incomplete data in real-world settings
  • Privacy-preserving training methods for sensitive health data

Real-World Data Challenges

Unlike previous research that used heavily curated datasets, our work addresses the challenges of real-world clinical data including missing values, inconsistent formatting, varying data quality, and the need to handle data from multiple healthcare systems with different practices and documentation standards.

Clinical Decision Support Integration

Our early detection system is designed as a clinical decision support tool that can be integrated into routine healthcare workflows. The system will provide risk scores and recommendations to clinicians during regular patient encounters, enabling proactive screening and intervention.

Clinical Validation Pipeline

The project will culminate in a prospective clinical trial to evaluate how our AI system performs in real-world settings when deployed to help clinicians identify high-risk patients. This validation study will assess not only diagnostic accuracy but also clinical utility, physician acceptance, and patient outcomes.

Implementation Considerations
  • User Interface Design - Intuitive dashboards for busy clinicians
  • Alert Fatigue Prevention - Calibrated thresholds to minimize false positives
  • Explainable AI - Clear reasoning for risk assessments
  • Privacy and Security - HIPAA-compliant implementation
  • Continuous Monitoring - Performance tracking and model updates

Project Timeline & Milestones

Integrated 5-Year Research Plan

This comprehensive research program spans multiple phases, from foundational model development through clinical validation, with parallel tracks for tumor evolution understanding and early detection implementation.
Year 1 (2025-2026)

Foundation & Baseline

Deploy evoCancerGPT for tumor evolution studies, validate existing early detection methodologies on institutional dataset, and establish baseline performance metrics for both research tracks.

Year 2 (2026-2027)

Algorithm Development & Integration

Develop multi-modal foundation models for genomics, create novel LLM architectures for EHR analysis, and integrate clinical trial data with evolution models.

Year 3 (2027-2028)

Validation & Optimization

Extensive validation of both tumor evolution predictions and early detection algorithms, implement RLHF enhancements, and develop clinical decision support interfaces.

Year 4 (2028-2029)

Clinical Integration

Pilot deployment of early detection system in clinical settings, integrate evolution insights into treatment planning, and collect prospective validation data.

Year 5 (2029-2030)

Prospective Clinical Trials

Large-scale clinical trials for early detection system, validation of evolution-based treatment strategies, and establishment of new standards for AI in cancer care.

Expected Impact & Future Directions

Transforming Cancer Care Through AI

Our integrated approach to cancer AI research bridges the gap between basic science and clinical application. By developing LLMs that can both understand tumor evolution and enable early detection, we are creating a comprehensive framework for AI-driven cancer care. This work has the potential to:

  • Increase pancreatic cancer survival rates from 12% to potentially 50-70% through early detection
  • Enable personalized treatment strategies based on predicted tumor evolution patterns
  • Establish new standards for AI integration in cancer research and clinical care
  • Create generalizable frameworks applicable to other cancer types and diseases
  • Position the Hale Center as a global leader in AI-driven cancer genomics

Next Phase Developments

We are expanding our GenAI capabilities to include mechanistic interpretability methods, reinforcement learning enhancements, and multi-institutional collaborations. Future work will focus on creating a unified AI platform that seamlessly integrates tumor evolution insights with clinical decision-making, ultimately transforming how we understand, detect, and treat cancer.
