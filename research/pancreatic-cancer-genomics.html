Pancreatic Cancer Genomics

Integrative immune modeling, drug resistance mechanisms, and target discovery through single-cell multi-omics, CRISPR screens, and clinical trial data analysis

Research Overview

Our laboratory conducts comprehensive genomic analyses of pancreatic cancer, integrating single-cell transcriptomics, T-cell receptor sequencing, and CRISPR screening to understand treatment response and resistance mechanisms. We analyze clinical trial data from the Hale Center to identify biomarkers for immunotherapy response, characterize resistance to KRAS inhibition, and discover novel therapeutic targets through surface proteome profiling.

Clinical Impact

Our integrated genomics approach directly impacts patient care by: (1) identifying biomarkers that predict immunotherapy response, (2) elucidating resistance mechanisms to guide combination therapies, (3) discovering novel antibody-drug conjugate targets, and (4) creating accessible data resources that accelerate translational research across the Hale Center.

Integrative Immune Modeling

Single-cell Analysis of Immunotherapy-treated Pancreatic Cancers

We analyze scRNA-seq and scTCR-seq data from DFCI clinical trials comparing FOLFIRINOX, pembrolizumab, and gemcitabine/nab-paclitaxel treatments. Our integrated analysis reveals treatment-specific immune responses and TCR clonal expansion patterns that correlate with clinical outcomes.

Collaboration: Dr. Chong Zuo (Dr. Stephanie Dougan's lab, DFCI)

Key Findings

  • FOLFIRINOX drives CD8 T effector memory (Tem) cell expansion
  • Pembrolizumab specifically affects CD8 T effector cells (Teff)
  • Cell2Sentence framework achieves 85% accuracy in cell-type classification
  • Treatment prediction accuracy reaches 95% using fine-tuned LLMs
Data Integration

Tumor and blood samples with paired TCR sequencing

LLM Application

Cell2Sentence and scGPT for cellular embeddings

Drug Perturbation & Resistance Mechanisms

Mechanisms of Resistance to KRAS Inhibition

Using clinical samples and preclinical models, we characterized heterogeneous genetic and non-genetic mechanisms of resistance to KRAS inhibitors. Our work guides combination therapy approaches to improve efficacy and durability of these promising treatments.

Major Discoveries

  • Mutations in PIK3CA and KRAS emerge at resistance
  • Amplifications of KRASG12C, MYC, MET, EGFR, CDK6
  • EMT and PI3K-AKT-mTOR signaling drive resistance
  • Mesenchymal/basal states show increased KRAS inhibitor response
  • Combination with chemotherapy improves tumor control
Model Systems

KPC mice, organoids, cell lines, PDX models

Drug Testing

Adagrasib, sotorasib, MRTX1133

Genomic Analysis

WES, RNA-seq, amplification detection

Dilly J et al. "Mechanisms of Resistance to Oncogenic KRAS Inhibition in Pancreatic Cancer" Cancer Discovery 2024

CRISPR Screening for Pan-RAS vs Mutant-Selective Inhibition

We are conducting genome-wide CRISPR screens to compare pan-RAS versus mutant-selective KRAS inhibitors across multiple pancreatic cancer cell lines. Our novel permutation-based analysis integrates multiple replicates to identify robust resistance and sensitization genes.

Collaboration: Dr. Andrew Aguirre's lab (DFCI/BWH)

Inhibitors Tested

RMC-7977 (pan-RAS), RMC-9945, MRTX1133 (G12D-specific)

Analysis Focus

Resistance genes, sensitizers, pathway modulation

Statistical Method

Permutation approach for replicate integration

Analysis Questions

  • Pan-RAS vs mutant-selective inhibitor differences
  • Universal vs specific resistance mechanisms
  • Common vulnerabilities across inhibitor types
  • Protein-protein interaction networks in resistance

PhageDisplay Antibody Screening for Novel Targets

We employ PhageDisplay technology to identify novel cell surface targets in KRAS inhibitor-resistant pancreatic cancer. Our VHH antibody library screening reveals targetable proteins for developing next-generation antibody-drug conjugates.

Experimental Design

5 conditions: parental/resistant, baseline/treated

Technology

VHH phage library, NGS sequencing

Analysis Platform

dBio Abgenesis for clonotype clustering

Screening Approach

  • Positive selection on pancreatic cancer cells
  • Negative selection on 293T cells
  • Multiple enrichment rounds
  • High-throughput sequencing of enriched VHH

Future Directions

We are expanding our pancreatic cancer genomics research to include: (1) multi-modal integration of spatial transcriptomics with immune profiling, (2) development of resistance prediction models using machine learning, (3) validation of novel ADC targets in preclinical models, and (4) prospective biomarker validation in clinical trials. Our goal is to translate genomic insights into actionable therapeutic strategies that improve patient outcomes.
