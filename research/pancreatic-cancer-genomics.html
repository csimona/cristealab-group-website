Pancreatic Cancer Genomics

Comprehensive genomic and immune profiling to understand pancreatic cancer biology, predict treatment responses, and develop novel therapeutic strategies through integrative analysis of multi-modal data from clinical trials and patient samples.

Research Overview

Our pancreatic cancer genomics research combines cutting-edge technologies in immune profiling, drug discovery, and computational analysis to tackle one of the most challenging cancers. We integrate single-cell technologies, CRISPR screening, proteomics, and AI-driven approaches to understand disease mechanisms and develop new therapeutic strategies.

Immunotherapy Revolution

While immunotherapy has transformed treatment for many cancers, pancreatic cancer remains largely resistant to these approaches. Our research aims to overcome this resistance by understanding the unique immune landscape of pancreatic tumors and identifying combination strategies that can make immunotherapy effective in this challenging cancer type.

KRAS: The "Undruggable" Target

KRAS has long been considered "undruggable" due to its smooth protein surface and high affinity for its substrate. Recent breakthroughs in KRAS inhibitor development have opened new therapeutic possibilities for pancreatic cancer patients, but resistance inevitably emerges. Our research aims to stay ahead of resistance by understanding its mechanisms and developing combination strategies to prevent or overcome it.

Immune Profiling & Surveillance

Integrative Analysis of Tumor and Blood

Immunotherapy-Treated Pancreatic Cancer Patients

We perform comprehensive integrative analysis of tumor tissue and peripheral blood from pancreatic cancer patients treated with immunotherapy. This dual-compartment approach reveals how systemic immune responses correlate with local tumor immune environments and treatment outcomes.
Multi-compartment Analysis
  • Simultaneous analysis of tumor-infiltrating and circulating immune cells
  • Longitudinal tracking of immune responses during treatment
  • Integration of single-cell transcriptomics with TCR repertoire data
  • Identification of shared vs. compartment-specific immune signatures
  • Correlation with clinical response and survival outcomes

Clinical Translation

This research enables the development of liquid biopsy-based biomarkers for immunotherapy response prediction. By analyzing peripheral blood samples, we can non-invasively monitor treatment responses and adjust therapeutic strategies in real-time, improving patient outcomes while reducing the need for invasive tumor biopsies.

Immunotherapy Liquid Biopsy Multi-compartment Analysis Longitudinal Profiling Biomarker Discovery

Foundation Models for Immunotherapy Response Prediction

We develop and apply foundation models for computational pathology to predict patient outcomes after treatment with immune checkpoint blockade. Using the ImmunoProfiler dataset from DFCI, we integrate histopathology images with immune profiling data to create comprehensive predictive models.
Computational Pathology Integration
  • Foundation model analysis of H&E stained tumor sections
  • Automated immune cell detection and quantification
  • Integration with molecular immune profiling data
  • Multi-modal predictive modeling for treatment response
  • Validation across multiple cancer types and treatment regimens

Drug Discovery & KRAS Inhibition

Mechanisms of KRAS Inhibition Resistance

Whole Exome Sequencing and Bulk Genomics Analysis

We perform comprehensive genomic analysis of pancreatic cancer models and patient samples that have developed resistance to KRAS inhibitors. This work identifies genetic alterations that drive resistance and reveals potential therapeutic targets to overcome resistance mechanisms.
Resistance Analysis Pipeline
  • Longitudinal whole exome sequencing of resistant tumor samples
  • Comparative genomics between sensitive and resistant cell lines
  • Identification of resistance-associated mutations and copy number alterations
  • Pathway analysis of resistance mechanisms
  • Validation of resistance drivers through functional studies

Key Resistance Mechanisms Identified

Our analysis has revealed multiple pathways through which pancreatic cancers can escape KRAS inhibition, including reactivation of downstream signaling pathways, activation of alternative oncogenic drivers, and metabolic reprogramming. Understanding these mechanisms enables the development of rational combination therapies.

CRISPR Screening of KRAS Inhibitors

Pan-RAS and KRAS Mutant-Selective Inhibition Analysis

We conduct large-scale CRISPR knockout screens to identify genetic dependencies and resistance mechanisms for both pan-RAS inhibitors and KRAS mutant-selective inhibitors in pancreatic cancer cell lines. This systematic approach reveals vulnerabilities that can be exploited for combination therapies.
CRISPR Screen Design
  • Genome-wide CRISPR knockout screens in multiple pancreatic cancer cell lines
  • Comparative analysis of pan-RAS vs. mutant-selective inhibitor responses
  • Time-course analysis to identify early and late resistance mechanisms
  • Drug dose response screening to identify synergistic combinations
  • Functional validation of top candidate genes

Target Discovery Through PhageDisplay and Mass Spectrometry

We employ complementary approaches of PhageDisplay antibody screening and quantitative mass spectrometry to identify novel cell surface targets and understand protein-level changes during KRAS inhibition and resistance development.
Integrated Discovery Pipeline
  • Large-scale phage display library screening on pancreatic cancer cell lines
  • Quantitative proteomics analysis under various drug concentrations
  • Time-course analysis of protein expression changes during treatment
  • Cell surface proteome enrichment for ADC target identification
  • Post-translational modification analysis (phosphoproteomics, ubiquitinomics)
  • Antibody affinity maturation and characterization

Novel Target Discovery

Our integrated approach has identified several previously unknown cell surface proteins that are specifically expressed or upregulated in KRAS-driven pancreatic cancers. These targets represent opportunities for developing highly specific antibody-drug conjugates that can deliver cytotoxic payloads directly to cancer cells.

Pancreatic Cancer Data Repository

Hale Center Clinical Trial Data Integration

We are building a comprehensive data repository that integrates multi-modal immune profiling data from ongoing clinical trials at the Hale Center for Pancreatic Cancer. This resource will serve as a foundation for AI-driven discovery and enable cross-trial comparisons and meta-analyses.
Repository Components
  • Standardized data collection protocols across clinical trials
  • Multi-modal data integration (genomics, proteomics, imaging)
  • Longitudinal patient tracking and outcome correlation
  • AI-ready data formatting and annotation
  • Secure, HIPAA-compliant data storage and access systems
  • Collaborative research platform for multi-institutional studies

Research Acceleration

This repository will dramatically accelerate pancreatic cancer research by providing researchers with access to high-quality, standardized datasets. The integration of multiple data types and clinical outcomes will enable machine learning approaches that would be impossible with smaller, single-trial datasets.

Clinical Trials Data Integration Standardization Multi-modal Data Research Infrastructure

Future Directions

Next-Generation Pancreatic Cancer Research

We are developing the next generation of integrative approaches that combine spatial omics, large language models, and foundation models for comprehensive understanding of pancreatic cancer biology. Our future work will focus on:

  • AI-driven prediction of drug responses and resistance evolution
  • Spatial multi-omics integration for tumor microenvironment analysis
  • Personalized combination therapy design using machine learning
  • Real-time treatment monitoring through liquid biopsy and AI analysis
  • Development of next-generation immunotherapies for pancreatic cancer
  • Creation of digital twins for patient-specific treatment optimization
