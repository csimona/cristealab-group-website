Immune Modeling & Immunotherapy

Integrative longitudinal immune modeling combining single cell transcriptomic and T-cell receptor sequencing clinical patient data to predict immunotherapy responses and understand immune evasion mechanisms.

Research Overview

Our immune modeling research integrates cutting-edge single-cell technologies with T-cell receptor sequencing to understand how pancreatic cancer evades immune surveillance and develops resistance to immunotherapy. We develop predictive models that can identify patients most likely to respond to immune checkpoint blockade and other immunotherapeutic approaches.

Immunotherapy Revolution

While immunotherapy has transformed treatment for many cancers, pancreatic cancer remains largely resistant to these approaches. Our research aims to overcome this resistance by understanding the unique immune landscape of pancreatic tumors and identifying combination strategies that can make immunotherapy effective in this challenging cancer type.

Project A: Integrative Analysis of Tumor and Blood

Immunotherapy-Treated Pancreatic Cancer Patients

We perform comprehensive integrative analysis of tumor tissue and peripheral blood from pancreatic cancer patients treated with immunotherapy. This dual-compartment approach reveals how systemic immune responses correlate with local tumor immune environments and treatment outcomes.
Multi-compartment Analysis
  • Simultaneous analysis of tumor-infiltrating and circulating immune cells
  • Longitudinal tracking of immune responses during treatment
  • Integration of single-cell transcriptomics with TCR repertoire data
  • Identification of shared vs. compartment-specific immune signatures
  • Correlation with clinical response and survival outcomes

Clinical Translation

This research enables the development of liquid biopsy-based biomarkers for immunotherapy response prediction. By analyzing peripheral blood samples, we can non-invasively monitor treatment responses and adjust therapeutic strategies in real-time, improving patient outcomes while reducing the need for invasive tumor biopsies.

Key Research Questions
  • How do systemic immune responses predict local tumor responses to immunotherapy?
  • Which circulating immune cell populations are most predictive of treatment success?
  • How does T-cell receptor diversity change during immunotherapy treatment?
  • Can peripheral blood signatures replace tumor biopsies for response prediction?

Project B: Pancreatic Cancer Data Repository

Hale Center Clinical Trial Data Integration

We are building a comprehensive data repository that integrates multi-modal immune profiling data from ongoing clinical trials at the Hale Center for Pancreatic Cancer. This resource will serve as a foundation for AI-driven discovery and enable cross-trial comparisons and meta-analyses.
Repository Components
  • Standardized data collection protocols across clinical trials
  • Multi-modal data integration (genomics, proteomics, imaging)
  • Longitudinal patient tracking and outcome correlation
  • AI-ready data formatting and annotation
  • Secure, HIPAA-compliant data storage and access systems
  • Collaborative research platform for multi-institutional studies

Research Acceleration

This repository will dramatically accelerate pancreatic cancer research by providing researchers with access to high-quality, standardized datasets. The integration of multiple data types and clinical outcomes will enable machine learning approaches that would be impossible with smaller, single-trial datasets.

Data Standards & Quality

We implement rigorous quality control measures and standardized protocols to ensure data consistency across different clinical trials and timepoints. This includes standardized sample collection, processing protocols, and bioinformatics pipelines that enable meaningful cross-study comparisons.

Project C: Predicting Immunotherapy Outcomes

Foundation Models for Immunotherapy Response Prediction

We develop and apply foundation models for computational pathology to predict patient outcomes after treatment with immune checkpoint blockade. Using the ImmunoProfiler dataset from DFCI, we integrate histopathology images with immune profiling data to create comprehensive predictive models.
Computational Pathology Integration
  • Foundation model analysis of H&E stained tumor sections
  • Automated immune cell detection and quantification
  • Integration with molecular immune profiling data
  • Multi-modal predictive modeling for treatment response
  • Validation across multiple cancer types and treatment regimens

Clinical Decision Support

Our predictive models will be integrated into clinical workflows to help oncologists make informed decisions about immunotherapy treatment. By analyzing routine pathology samples with AI, we can provide rapid, accurate predictions of treatment response, enabling personalized treatment selection and improved patient outcomes.

Model Development & Validation

We employ rigorous machine learning practices including cross-validation, external validation cohorts, and prospective validation studies. Our models are designed to be robust across different institutions and patient populations, ensuring broad clinical applicability.

ImmunoProfiler Dataset Integration

The ImmunoProfiler dataset from DFCI represents one of the largest collections of immune checkpoint blockade-treated patients with comprehensive molecular and clinical annotation. Our analysis of this dataset provides unprecedented insights into the mechanisms of immunotherapy response and resistance.

Methodological Innovations

Advanced Computational Approaches

Our research develops novel computational methods specifically designed for immune system analysis in cancer. These methodological innovations enable more accurate and comprehensive understanding of tumor-immune interactions and treatment responses.
Novel Analytical Methods
  • Multi-modal data integration algorithms for immune profiling
  • Longitudinal modeling of immune system dynamics
  • Network analysis of T-cell receptor repertoire evolution
  • Machine learning models for immune signature discovery
  • Causal inference methods for treatment effect estimation
Technical Challenges & Solutions

Immune system analysis presents unique computational challenges including high-dimensional data integration, temporal dynamics modeling, and rare cell population detection. Our methodological innovations address these challenges through specialized algorithms and statistical approaches designed for immune system complexity.
