Drug Discovery & KRAS Inhibition

Pioneering drug perturbation analysis through CRISPR screens and cell surfaceome analysis to identify novel therapeutic targets and overcome resistance to KRAS inhibitors in pancreatic cancer.

Research Overview

Our drug discovery research focuses on understanding and overcoming resistance to KRAS inhibitors, one of the most promising recent advances in pancreatic cancer treatment. We employ cutting-edge CRISPR screening technologies and proteomics approaches to identify vulnerabilities in KRAS-driven cancers and develop next-generation therapeutic strategies.

KRAS: The "Undruggable" Target

KRAS has long been considered "undruggable" due to its smooth protein surface and high affinity for its substrate. Recent breakthroughs in KRAS inhibitor development have opened new therapeutic possibilities for pancreatic cancer patients, but resistance inevitably emerges. Our research aims to stay ahead of resistance by understanding its mechanisms and developing combination strategies to prevent or overcome it.

Project A: Mechanisms of KRAS Inhibition Resistance

Whole Exome Sequencing and Bulk Genomics Analysis

We perform comprehensive genomic analysis of pancreatic cancer models and patient samples that have developed resistance to KRAS inhibitors. This work identifies genetic alterations that drive resistance and reveals potential therapeutic targets to overcome resistance mechanisms.
Resistance Analysis Pipeline
  • Longitudinal whole exome sequencing of resistant tumor samples
  • Comparative genomics between sensitive and resistant cell lines
  • Identification of resistance-associated mutations and copy number alterations
  • Pathway analysis of resistance mechanisms
  • Validation of resistance drivers through functional studies

Key Resistance Mechanisms Identified

Our analysis has revealed multiple pathways through which pancreatic cancers can escape KRAS inhibition, including reactivation of downstream signaling pathways, activation of alternative oncogenic drivers, and metabolic reprogramming. Understanding these mechanisms enables the development of rational combination therapies.

Clinical Translation

We work closely with clinical teams to analyze samples from patients who develop resistance to KRAS inhibitors. This real-world data validation ensures that our laboratory findings are clinically relevant and can inform patient treatment decisions.

Project B: CRISPR Screening of KRAS Inhibitors

Pan-RAS and KRAS Mutant-Selective Inhibition Analysis

We conduct large-scale CRISPR knockout screens to identify genetic dependencies and resistance mechanisms for both pan-RAS inhibitors and KRAS mutant-selective inhibitors in pancreatic cancer cell lines. This systematic approach reveals vulnerabilities that can be exploited for combination therapies.
CRISPR Screen Design
  • Genome-wide CRISPR knockout screens in multiple pancreatic cancer cell lines
  • Comparative analysis of pan-RAS vs. mutant-selective inhibitor responses
  • Time-course analysis to identify early and late resistance mechanisms
  • Drug dose response screening to identify synergistic combinations
  • Functional validation of top candidate genes

Therapeutic Innovation

Our CRISPR screens have identified several promising combination therapy opportunities that are now being tested in preclinical models and clinical trials. These rational combinations have the potential to prevent resistance and improve patient outcomes compared to KRAS inhibitor monotherapy.

Project A: PhageDisplay Antibody Screening

Large-scale Antibody Screening for KRAS Inhibition

We employ PhageDisplay technology to screen large antibody libraries for novel binding partners and therapeutic targets in KRAS-driven pancreatic cancer. This approach enables the discovery of cell surface proteins that can be targeted with antibody-drug conjugates or immunotherapies.
PhageDisplay Screening Pipeline
  • Large-scale phage display library screening on pancreatic cancer cell lines
  • Differential screening between KRAS mutant and wild-type cells
  • Identification of cell surface proteins enriched in resistant populations
  • Antibody affinity maturation and characterization
  • Functional validation of antibody-target interactions

Novel Target Discovery

PhageDisplay screening has identified several previously unknown cell surface proteins that are specifically expressed or upregulated in KRAS-driven pancreatic cancers. These targets represent opportunities for developing highly specific antibody-drug conjugates that can deliver cytotoxic payloads directly to cancer cells.

Antibody-Drug Conjugate Development

Selected antibodies from our screens are being developed into antibody-drug conjugates (ADCs) in collaboration with pharmaceutical partners. This approach allows for targeted delivery of potent cytotoxic agents while minimizing systemic toxicity.

Project B: Mass Spectrometry Target Identification

Proteomics Analysis of KRAS Inhibition Resistance

We use quantitative mass spectrometry to identify proteins that are differentially expressed in response to KRAS inhibition and during the development of resistance. This proteomics approach reveals post-translational modifications and protein-level changes that may not be detected through genomic or transcriptomic analyses.
Proteomics Pipeline
  • Quantitative proteomics analysis under various drug concentrations
  • Time-course analysis of protein expression changes during treatment
  • Comparative analysis of sensitive vs. resistant cell populations
  • Post-translational modification analysis (phosphoproteomics, ubiquitinomics)
  • Cell surface proteome enrichment for ADC target identification
Resistance Biomarker Discovery

Our mass spectrometry analysis has identified protein signatures that can predict resistance to KRAS inhibitors before it becomes clinically apparent. These biomarkers could enable early intervention with combination therapies to prevent resistance development.

Drug Target Validation

Proteins identified through mass spectrometry are validated as potential drug targets through functional studies, including CRISPR knockout, overexpression experiments, and small molecule inhibition. This multi-level validation ensures that identified targets are therapeutically relevant.

Translational Applications

From Discovery to Clinical Translation

Our drug discovery research is designed with clinical translation in mind. We work closely with pharmaceutical companies, clinical investigators, and regulatory agencies to ensure that our discoveries can be rapidly translated into new treatments for patients.
Clinical Collaboration
  • Partnership with Revolution Medicines for RMC-6236 clinical trial analysis
  • Collaboration with biotech companies for ADC development
  • Integration with ongoing clinical trials at Dana-Farber
  • Biomarker validation in patient samples
  • Regulatory guidance for companion diagnostic development

Patient Impact

Several targets and biomarkers identified through our research are now being tested in clinical trials. This direct translational pathway ensures that our discoveries can rapidly benefit pancreatic cancer patients, who have limited treatment options and urgent need for new therapeutic approaches.
