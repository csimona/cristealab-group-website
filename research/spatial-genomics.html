Spatial Modeling and Analyses of Pancreatic Cancer

Pioneering spatial transcriptomics and proteomics analyses to understand tumor microenvironment architecture, fibroblast heterogeneity, and resistance mechanisms in pancreatic cancer

Research Overview

Our laboratory is at the forefront of spatial genomics technology, developing comprehensive analysis frameworks for CosMx spatial transcriptomics and high-plex protein imaging. We are analyzing over 300 pancreatic tumors to map the complex relationships between spatial organization, cellular composition, and clinical outcomes. Our work reveals how the tumor microenvironment architecture drives treatment resistance and identifies targetable spatial niches for therapeutic intervention.

Clinical Translation

Our spatial analysis pipelines are actively used for multiple clinical trials at the Hale Center, including the Break Through Cancer Trial and RMC-6236 RAS inhibitor trial. These tools enable: (1) automated processing of spatial transcriptomics data, (2) identification of resistance mechanisms to KRAS inhibition, (3) mapping of fibroblast heterogeneity and its impact on treatment response, and (4) development of spatial biomarkers for patient stratification.

Key Projects

CosMx Spatial Transcriptomics Analysis Framework

We developed a comprehensive automated pipeline for CosMx NanoString spatial transcriptomics data analysis, supporting both 1,000 and 6,000 gene panels. The pipeline performs cell segmentation, quality control, tumor polarity analysis, cell subtyping, and fibroblast activity assessment. Built using Snakemake, it requires no coding experience and ensures full reproducibility.

Cell segmentation, QC metrics, and batch effect correction

Fibroblast Spatial Diversity in Primary and Metastatic PDAC

In collaboration with Drs. Wolpin and Nowak, we spatially profiled 300+ pancreatic tumors using 12-marker immunofluorescence to map fibroblast heterogeneity and its clinical impact. We identified distinct fibroblast subtypes with prognostic significance and characterized their response to neoadjuvant FOLFIRINOX chemotherapy.

Major Findings

  • FAP+ fibroblasts associated with poor survival and basal tumors
  • Inflammatory CAFs (iCAFs) correlated with better clinical outcomes
  • FOLFIRINOX treatment remodels fibroblast composition
  • Distinct ligand-receptor patterns between tumor subtypes
Clinical Cohorts

Upfront surgery, neoadjuvant therapy, liver metastases

Spatial Analysis

Cell density, composition, tumor-fibroblast interactions

HexAligner - Collagen-Stroma Spatial Analysis

We developed HexAligner, a novel computational tool to analyze spatial relationships between collagen deposition and fibroblast subtypes. The tool aligns multi-modal images and tessellates them into hexagonal regions for quantitative analysis of stromal architecture.

Key Discovery

  • Myofibroblasts (myCAFs) concentrate with collagen deposits
  • iCAFs show less collagen association
  • Implications for drug delivery and immune infiltration
  • Open-source tool for spatial image analysis

Spatial Features of KRAS Inhibitor Resistance

Using CosMx spatial transcriptomics, we are characterizing resistance mechanisms to the KRASG12D inhibitor MRTX1133 in mouse models. Our analysis reveals malignant subclones with distinct spatial niches and identifies putative drivers of resistance in their microenvironmental context.

Model System

High-plex Protein Profiling for RAS Inhibitor Clinical Trial

In collaboration with Revolution Medicines and Breakthrough Cancer, we are analyzing 60-marker protein expression profiles from the RMC-6236 clinical trial. Our computational pipeline uses distribution-based thresholding and machine learning to accurately phenotype cells and track treatment-induced changes in the tumor microenvironment.

Technical Innovation

  • 60 patients at 3 timepoints with 60-plex protein profiling
  • Automated thresholding validated against pathologist annotations
  • High-confidence phenotyping for biomarker discovery
  • Spatial analysis of treatment response patterns

Counterfactual Perturbations with Pathology Foundation Models

We are developing explainable AI methods to generate synthetic H&E images that simulate post-immunotherapy tumor morphology. Using the ImmunoPROFILE dataset (1,767 images), we create counterfactual perturbations that predict treatment response while maintaining biological interpretability.

Future Directions

We are expanding our spatial genomics capabilities to include: (1) integration with single-cell foundation models for enhanced cell type annotation, (2) development of spatial biomarkers for early detection, (3) multi-modal integration of spatial transcriptomics with proteomics and metabolomics, and (4) AI-driven discovery of targetable spatial niches. Our goal is to transform spatial biology data into actionable insights for precision oncology.
