Early Detection AI

Developing Large Language Model methodology for early detection of pancreatic cancer from electronic health record data using deep learning clinical decision support tools.

Research Overview

Pancreatic cancer is one of the most lethal cancers, largely due to late-stage diagnosis when treatment options are limited. Our early detection research leverages the power of Large Language Models and deep learning to identify high-risk patients years before clinical symptoms appear, using electronic health record data from routine healthcare interactions.

The Pancreatic Cancer Early Detection Challenge

Pancreatic cancer has a 5-year survival rate of only 12%, primarily because 80% of patients are diagnosed at advanced stages when surgical resection is no longer possible. Early detection could dramatically improve outcomes, as patients diagnosed at early stages have survival rates approaching 70%. However, pancreatic cancer is often called "silent" because it produces few symptoms until advanced stages.

Transformative Potential

This research has the potential to transform pancreatic cancer outcomes by identifying patients at high risk 1-3 years before clinical diagnosis. Early identification would enable enhanced surveillance, early intervention, and potentially preventive treatments, fundamentally changing the trajectory of this devastating disease.

Electronic Health Record Data

We have access to electronic health records from 1 million patients in the Mass Gen-Brigham-Dana Farber medical system, representing one of the largest and most comprehensive healthcare datasets available for pancreatic cancer research. LLM Methodology Development

Adapting Foundation Models for EHR Analysis

Led by Dr. Jifan Gao (joining June 2025), we are developing novel Large Language Model architectures specifically designed for electronic health record analysis. This work builds on recent breakthroughs in medical AI while addressing the unique challenges of pancreatic cancer early detection.
Technical Innovation
  • Adaptation of transformer architectures for temporal medical data
  • Multi-modal integration of text, numerical, and imaging data
  • Attention mechanisms for long-term temporal relationships
  • Handling missing and incomplete data in real-world settings
  • Privacy-preserving training methods for sensitive health data

Real-World Data Challenges

Unlike previous research that used heavily curated datasets, our work addresses the challenges of real-world clinical data including missing values, inconsistent formatting, varying data quality, and the need to handle data from multiple healthcare systems with different practices and documentation standards.

Foundation Model Baseline

We begin by implementing and validating the methodology from Placido et al. (Nature Medicine 2023) on our institutional dataset. This provides a benchmark for performance comparison and helps identify areas where our novel approaches can improve upon existing methods.

Clinical Decision Support Integration

Deployment in Healthcare Workflows

Our early detection system is designed as a clinical decision support tool that can be integrated into routine healthcare workflows. The system will provide risk scores and recommendations to clinicians during regular patient encounters, enabling proactive screening and intervention.
Clinical Integration Features
  • Real-time risk assessment during patient visits
  • Integration with electronic health record systems
  • Automated alerts for high-risk patients
  • Evidence-based recommendations for follow-up care
  • Continuous learning from new patient data

Clinical Validation Pipeline

The project will culminate in a prospective clinical trial to evaluate how our AI system performs in real-world settings when deployed to help clinicians identify high-risk patients. This validation study will assess not only diagnostic accuracy but also clinical utility, physician acceptance, and patient outcomes.

Implementation Considerations
  • User Interface Design - Intuitive dashboards for busy clinicians
  • Alert Fatigue Prevention - Calibrated thresholds to minimize false positives
  • Explainable AI - Clear reasoning for risk assessments
  • Privacy and Security - HIPAA-compliant implementation
  • Continuous Monitoring - Performance tracking and model updates

Project Timeline & Milestones

5-Year Research Plan

This ambitious project is structured as a comprehensive 5-year research program, from initial methodology development through clinical validation and deployment. Each phase builds on previous results while addressing specific technical and clinical challenges.
Year 1 (2025-2026)

Foundation & Baseline

Validate existing methodologies on institutional dataset, establish baseline performance, and identify key challenges in real-world data application.

Year 2 (2026-2027)

Algorithm Development

Develop novel LLM architectures adapted for pancreatic cancer early detection, implement multi-modal data integration, and optimize for missing data handling.

Year 3 (2027-2028)

Validation & Optimization

Extensive validation on held-out datasets, performance optimization, and development of clinical decision support interface.

Year 4 (2028-2029)

Clinical Integration

Pilot deployment in clinical settings, physician training, workflow integration, and collection of prospective validation data.

Year 5 (2029-2030)

Prospective Clinical Trial

Large-scale prospective clinical trial to evaluate real-world performance, patient outcomes, and clinical utility of the early detection system.

Expected Impact & Innovation

Transforming Pancreatic Cancer Outcomes

This research represents a paradigm shift in pancreatic cancer care, moving from reactive treatment of advanced disease to proactive identification and intervention for high-risk patients. The potential impact extends beyond pancreatic cancer to other challenging-to-detect cancers.

Clinical Impact

Early detection could increase pancreatic cancer survival rates from 12% to potentially 50-70% by enabling treatment at resectable stages. For a disease that affects 60,000+ Americans annually, this could save tens of thousands of lives each year and dramatically reduce healthcare costs associated with advanced cancer care.

Scientific Innovation
  • Methodological Advances - Novel LLM architectures for medical data
  • Real-World Application - First large-scale EHR-based pancreatic cancer prediction
  • Clinical Translation - Direct pathway from research to patient care
  • Generalizable Framework - Applicable to other cancer types

Broader Applications

The methodologies developed for pancreatic cancer early detection will be applicable to other cancers and diseases that benefit from early intervention. This research establishes a framework for AI-driven preventive medicine that could transform healthcare from reactive to proactive care.
