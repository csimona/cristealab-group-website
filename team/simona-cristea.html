Dr. Simona Cristea

Head of Genomics Data Science and AI, Hale Center for Pancreatic Cancer

Dr. Simona Cristea is Head of Genomics Data Science and AI at the Hale Center for Pancreatic Cancer and a faculty member in the Department of Data Science at Dana-Farber Cancer Institute, with a Research Scientist appointment at Harvard T.H. Chan School of Public Health. She drives the vision, innovation and strategic development of computational efforts to cure pancreatic cancer.

Research Interests

Large Language Models in Cancer Genomics

Developing single cell LLMs to understand and generate cancer progression and clinical responses using generative AI. Creating foundation models like evoCancerGPT for zero-shot cancer evolution prediction.

Spatial Genomics & AI

Spatial modeling and analyses of pancreatic cancer progression using clinical trial patient genomic and imaging data. Integrating CosMx spatial transcriptomics with computational pathology.

Immune Modeling & Immunotherapy

Integrative longitudinal immune modeling of single cell transcriptomic and TCR sequencing clinical patient data. Predicting patient outcomes after treatment with immune checkpoint blockade.

Drug Discovery & KRAS Inhibition

Drug perturbation analysis through CRISPR screens on KRAS inhibitors. Cell surfaceome analysis to identify targets for designing novel antibody-drug conjugates.

Early Detection AI

Developing LLM methodology for early detection of pancreatic cancer from electronic health record data using deep learning clinical decision support tools.

Breast Cancer Genomics

Spatial and temporal single cell analyses of breast tumor initiation, malignant transformations, and clinical response prediction for triple-negative breast cancers.

Spatial Transcriptomics

Integrating spatial and molecular information to understand the tumor microenvironment and cell-cell interactions in cancer progression.
