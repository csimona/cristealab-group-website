About Dr. Simona Cristea

Dr. Simona Cristea is Head of Genomics Data Science and AI at the Hale Center for Pancreatic Cancer and a faculty member in the Department of Data Science at Dana-Farber Cancer Institute, with a Research Scientist appointment at Harvard T.H. Chan School of Public Health.

She drives the vision, innovation and strategic development of Hale Center's computational efforts to cure pancreatic cancer. Her lab integrates AI, computational biology, genomics and evolution to understand and ultimately alter the molecular processes that initiate and drive cancer. Current work focuses on developing single cell large language models to understand and generate cancer progression and clinical responses using generative AI methodologies.

Active Projects

Research Areas

Large Language Models in Cancer Genomics

Developing single cell LLMs to understand and generate cancer progression and clinical responses using generative AI. Creating foundation models like evoCancerGPT for zero-shot cancer evolution prediction.

Learn More

Spatial Genomics & AI

Spatial modeling and analyses of pancreatic cancer progression using clinical trial patient genomic and imaging data. Integrating CosMx spatial transcriptomics with computational pathology and foundation models.

Learn More

Immune Modeling & Immunotherapy

Integrative longitudinal immune modeling of single cell transcriptomic and TCR sequencing clinical patient data. Predicting patient outcomes after treatment with immune checkpoint blockade.

Learn More

Drug Discovery & KRAS Inhibition

Drug perturbation analysis through CRISPR screens on KRAS inhibitors. Cell surfaceome analysis to identify targets for designing novel antibody-drug conjugates in pancreatic cancer.

Learn More

Early Detection AI

Developing LLM methodology for early detection of pancreatic cancer from electronic health record data in the Mass Gen-Brigham-Dana Farber medical system using deep learning clinical decision support tools.

Learn More

Breast Cancer Genomics

Spatial and temporal single cell analyses of breast tumor initiation, malignant transformations, and clinical response prediction for triple-negative breast cancers treated with antibody-drug conjugates.
