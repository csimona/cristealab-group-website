About Us

Dr. Simona Cristea is Head of Genomics Data Science and AI at the Hale Center for Pancreatic Cancer and a faculty member in the Department of Data Science at Dana-Farber Cancer Institute, with a Research Scientist appointment at Harvard T.H. Chan School of Public Health.

She drives the vision, innovation and strategic development of Hale Center's computational efforts to cure pancreatic cancer. Her lab integrates AI, computational biology, genomics and evolution to understand and ultimately alter the molecular processes that initiate and drive cancer. Current work focuses on developing single cell large language models to understand and generate cancer progression and clinical responses using generative AI methodologies.

Research Areas

GenAI Methods for Tumor Evolution and Early Detection

Developing genomics single cell foundation models to explain and generate tumor evolution and LLM methodology for pancreatic cancer early detection from electronic health record data in the Mass General-Brigham-Dana Farber medical system using deep learning clinical decision support tools.

Learn More

Spatial Progression of Pancreatic Tumors

Spatial Transcriptomics & Spatial Proteomics modeling and analyses of pancreatic cancer progression using clinical trial patient genomic and imaging data. Integrating CosMx spatial transcriptomics with computational pathology and foundation models.

Learn More

Pancreatic Cancer Genomics

Integrative longitudinal immune modeling of single cell transcriptomic and TCR sequencing clinical patient data for cancer surveillance and predicting patient outcomes after immunotherapy treatment. Drug perturbation analysis through CRISPR screens on KRAS inhibitors and cell surfaceome analysis to identify targets for novel antibody-drug conjugates.

Learn More

Breast Cancer Genomics

Spatial and temporal single cell analyses of breast tumor initiation, malignant transformations, and clinical response prediction for triple-negative breast cancers treated with antibody-drug conjugates.
